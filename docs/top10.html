Countries at Risk

Top 10 Countries

In the video above, we can see the timelapse of new cases by country. As it first peaked in China, many other countries have been severely impacted such as the United States, Italy, and Spain, with the US leading the total number of confirmed cases at over 350K as of April 7th. However, to truly understand the impact the novel coronavirus has, we want to take a deeper dive into a few other countrywide metrics.

Rate of Increase

The above visualization shows the number of new cases compared to the total confirmed cases of a country. The dotted line represents the average growth in cases over time. The countries at the most risk are above the line as they are seeing on average more new cases than the countries below the line. This indicates that the countries above the line have not yet seen the peak of infection and are at risk for even more infections.

Comparing the Top 10 Countries

This visualization shows the distributions of mortality (in light blue) to total confirmed cases (in dark blue). Hover over each country to see additional stats regarding each one. What we can learn from this is that even though the number of confirmed cases are very high for the US, the overall percent of the population infection is minimal. Compared to Italy, where there are less confirmed cases but a high percent of the population is impacted. Size is relative as different countries have different population sizes. Percent Infected

This visualization provides a distribution of countries based on the number of confirmed cases and the percent infected rate. It would be insensitive to say that we should only look at percent infected or vice versa, but this provides a clearer picture of the impact it has on any one country. Countries that have higher percent infected need stricter measures to flatten the curve as these countries' healthcare systems would most likely be inequiped with the demand. Similarly, countries with high total confirmed cases can also overwhelm health care systems even though the sentiment of the extent virus to the general public may be very limited.
