Country-Level Statistics

Overview

According to NPR , Covid-19 is not mutating very fast compared to other viruses. Because of this, the differences in spread rate and fatalities between countries should be due to some other variable. This visualization attempts to find correlations between other country-level variables and Covid-19 cases, recoveries, and deaths.

Correlations Dashboard
Early Insights

Covid-19 appears to be spreading faster and is more fatal in developed countries.

The average number of phones per 1000 people is positively correlated with spread while average birthrate is negatively correlated with spread. Average number of phones and average birthrate are both proxies for income and development of country; i.e. more developed countries with higher income per person tend to have higher number of cases and deaths per capita (Price 2013).

Space between people is correlated with slower spread rates.

Covid-19 confirmed cases is negatively correlated with average area per person and positively correlated with population density. Basically, countries that are more spread out with more area between people tend to have lower spread rates while densely populated countries tend to have higher spread rates. This could be some evidence towards social distancing practices.
