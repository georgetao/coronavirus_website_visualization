Country-Level Statistics

Overview

Why we should be taking social distancing more seriously.


According to NPR , Covid-19 is not mutating very fast compared to other viruses. Because of this, the differences in spread rate and fatalities between countries should be due to some other variable. This visualization attempts to find correlations between other country-level variables and Covid-19 cases, recoveries, and deaths.

Explanation

Coronavirus has hit wealthy and developed countries the hardest.

The rate of Covid-19 fatalities per capita is positively correlated with the average number of phones per capita. This indicates, the more wealthy an area is, the more likely it is to have a higher Coronavirus fatality rate. Another indication of this, is the fact that Covid-19 fatality rates are negatively correlated with average birthrate per captia. Poorer areas with less resources tend to have higher average birthrates, thus, lower birthrates indicate more wealthy countries. One potential reason why is because people in developed/wealthier countries tend to live closer together, often in urban areas.



Space between people is correlated with lower spread rates and lower fatalities per capita.

Countries that are more spread out with more area between people tend to have lower spread rates while densely populated countries tend to have higher spread rates. This indicates space between people is vital in slowing down the spread of Covid-19.
