Comparing COVID-19 and SARS

The goal of these comparisons is to see if we can make any predictions about COVID-19 by looking at trends from other pandemics. We chose to primarily look at the SARS outbreak in 2003 because it is the the most genetically similar to COVID-19.

It is clear that in every country excluding Taiwan, the magnitude of COVID-19 is larger than SARS. Looking at the global trends, we can see that the growth of SARS dropped significantly around 40 days in, while the growth of COVID-19 is still growing exponentially to this day (day 70).
